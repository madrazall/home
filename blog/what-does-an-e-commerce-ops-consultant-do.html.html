What Does an E-Commerce Ops Consultant Actually Do?

By Madrazall Built  ·  E-Commerce Operations  ·  5 min read

Most small e-commerce businesses have a front-end problem and a back-end problem. The front-end problem — traffic, ads, conversions — gets all the attention. The back-end problem quietly eats your margins, burns your team out, and eventually costs you customers.

That back-end problem is what an e-commerce ops consultant fixes.

"Your store can be converting at 4% and still hemorrhage money if the systems behind it are broken."

The Short Answer

An e-commerce ops consultant audits and fixes the operational systems that run your store — the stuff customers never see but feel immediately when it breaks. Shipping software. Inventory tracking. Order routing. Platform integrations. Fulfillment workflows. Reporting.

Where a marketing consultant asks "how do we get more people to buy?" — an ops consultant asks "what happens after they buy, and why is it breaking?"

What They Actually Work On

Shipping & Fulfillment Systems

Tools like ShipStation, Shippo, and EasyPost are powerful — and commonly misconfigured. An ops consultant digs into your rate settings, carrier integrations, automation rules, and fulfillment workflows to find where orders are getting delayed, mislabeled, or shipped at the wrong rate.

Real example: a store processing 300 orders a month had 173 re-ship orders buried in their data — that's 57% of orders touching fulfillment twice. One audit, one fix.

Platform Integrations

Magento, Shopify, WooCommerce — these platforms don't always play nicely with shipping tools, inventory systems, or third-party apps out of the box. An ops consultant maps the data flow between your tools and patches the gaps where orders fall through.

Inventory & SKU Management

Wrong counts, duplicate SKUs, missing product data, import errors. If your team is constantly fighting inventory discrepancies, that's an ops problem — not a staffing problem.

Reporting & Visibility

Most small e-commerce businesses are flying blind. An ops consultant builds the reports that tell you what's actually happening: which SKUs are moving, where shipping costs are spiking, what your year-over-year order trends look like.

Automation

Anything your team does manually on repeat — order tagging, low-stock alerts, carrier selection, packing slip generation — can likely be automated. Ops consultants identify the highest-value manual tasks and build lightweight automations that save real hours each week.

Signs You Need One

  • Your order volume is growing but your team is working harder, not smarter
  • Shipping costs are unpredictable month to month
  • You're manually correcting the same types of errors repeatedly
  • Inventory counts never match what's actually on the shelf
  • You don't have reliable data on what's selling and what's not
  • A new tool was "set up" but nobody's sure if it's configured correctly
  • Your team avoids certain workflows because they're too complicated

What It's Not

An ops consultant is not a virtual assistant, a warehouse manager, or a developer building you a new platform from scratch. The work is investigative and systematic — auditing what exists, finding what's broken, and fixing or automating it within your current stack wherever possible.

The goal is always the same: your systems should run your store. Your team should run your business.

Frequently Asked
What does an e-commerce ops consultant do?
An e-commerce ops consultant audits and fixes the backend systems that run your online store — shipping software, inventory management, order automation, and platform integrations. They identify where orders are getting lost, where money is being wasted, and where manual work can be automated.
When do I need an e-commerce operations consultant?
Most small e-commerce businesses hit an ops wall between 100–500 orders per month. If your team is doing too much manually, shipping costs are unpredictable, or inventory is constantly wrong — that's the signal.
What's the difference between an e-commerce consultant and an ops consultant?
A general e-commerce consultant focuses on marketing, growth, or storefront design. An ops consultant focuses on the operational back-end: fulfillment, shipping integrations, inventory systems, and automation. Ops fixes what's breaking so growth work actually sticks.
What tools does an e-commerce ops consultant work with?
Common tools include ShipStation, Magento, Shopify, WooCommerce, Shippo, and custom Python or spreadsheet-based reporting. A good ops consultant works within your existing stack rather than forcing a migration.
